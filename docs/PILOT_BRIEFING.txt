CIVIL OPERATIONS - PILOT BRIEFING
(paste this into the Mission Editor briefing panel, trim what you do not use)

SITUATION
You fly for the regional emergency service. There is no enemy: your
opponents are time, weather and the terrain. Tasking is generated live
during the session, either automatically or by the emergency command
center. Every callout is broadcast with a severity from 1 to 10: severity
drives how hard the job is and how many points it is worth. You choose
what to answer: nothing is assigned.

YOUR RADIO MENU
Everything runs through F10 -> Civil Missions:
- Session leaderboard: live standings for everyone in the session.
- Firefighting: start water pickup, drop water, list active fires.
- Firefighting C-130: load retardant (at the reload apron), start line drop.
- Rescue: request a signal from the subject, list active rescue events.
- Police / SWAT: board a SWAT team at the base, check team status.
- Cargo transport: change the tier of a nearby load, list active pickups.
- Request illumination on nearest objective: night only, see NIGHT OPS.

TASK TYPES
WILDFIRE. Fires grow from small to raging (severity 10) and spread. Ground
crews respond on their own but they are slow: helicopters pick up water
hovering over a water point, the C-130 reloads retardant on the ground and
drops it in a line run at 150-250 m AGL, or airdrops retardant drums.
Forest fires burn bright; a landfill fire is a slow dark smoke column; an
industrial fire grows fast. The report tells you which one you are facing.

SEARCH AND RESCUE (mountain and sea). A subject is reported with a ROUGH
position and an approximate search circle on the F10 map. The exact
position is released only when a spotter airplane flies over the area and
identifies the subject, so a C-130 on station is worth a lot. Extraction:
hold a steady hover over the subject; sloppy hovering slows the timer and
the window is not forever. At sea, rescue vessels also steam toward the
search area: if you are too slow they may finish the job, and the credit
goes to the spotter.

MEDEVAC AND BATTLEFIELD CASEVAC. A casualty with a criticality clock.
High severity means a short clock: check the minutes in the callout before
you commit. Pick up with a steady hover, then deliver to any hospital pad
(hold low and still for 15 seconds inside the pad zone) or to the hospital
ship, which works even while she is underway.

POLICE. Chase: keep your helicopter close to the fleeing vehicle to build
pressure; lose contact and the pressure decays. At 100 percent the ground
units make the arrest. High severity fugitives are faster and may run in a
two-car convoy. SWAT: board a team at the SWAT base (the callout tells you
how many operators the job needs), then hold a hover over the objective to
fast-rope them in.

CARGO. Loads come in fixed mass tiers: check the warning when you arrive,
some loads need a heavy-lift helicopter. You can re-rig a load to another
tier via F10, it costs a re-rigging delay. Every load has a delivery
priority and an expiry time: priority 10 pays best and vanishes fastest.
Sling it to the destination zone. The C-130 can also airdrop supply crates
into the destination zone.

AVIATION TASKS
Recon: an anomaly is reported along the inspection corridor. Fly the line
low (below 300 m AGL), watch for the nudge message, then report it from
overhead via F10 -> Aviation tasks. VIP shuttle: board the passenger at
the announced pad (land and hold 20 seconds), fly them to the destination
pad; every hard maneuver costs comfort, and comfort is your score. Media:
hold 1-3 km from an active event, above 100 m, for 5 minutes and your
footage airs for points.

FIXED-WING PILOTS (Bronco, MB-339, L-39, C-101, Yak-52...): every airplane
can spot. Orbit a fire region to relay fire positions, overfly a rescue
search area to identify the subject (that releases the exact coordinates
to everyone and pays you spotter points). Recon, media and VIP shuttle
(pads on airfield aprons) are yours too. On fires you are the AIR ATTACK:
you do not haul retardant, you get below 600 m near the fire and
smoke-mark it via F10; for the next 5 minutes every drop on that fire
scores +25% and you earn the assist if it goes out. Direct the traffic.

SIGNALS AND NIGHT OPS
Ask the subject for a signal when you are within 15 km: orange smoke by
day, green flares by night. At night you also have one illumination flare
request every 10 minutes: it ignites over the nearest active objective and
the message gives you bearing and range. Unidentified rescue subjects only
get it over the approximate area: bring a spotter.

SCORING
Points = task value x quality x time x severity. Doing a hard job well and
fast beats farming easy ones. The leaderboard is live and resets at
mission end.
